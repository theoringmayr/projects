Theodor Ringmayr

Dirtywork

[Hero shot of branded truck parked in front of a well-maintained garden, golden hour lighting]

When a friend decided to leave his desk job to work with his hands, he thought he just needed a logo. But starting a landscaping business in Brandenburg meant more than good work—it meant looking the part from day one.

[Screenshot collage of 4-5 local competitor websites/business cards showing dated, unprofessional design]

The local landscaping scene told its own story. Pixelated logos, clipart, and Comic Sans dominated. For homeowners looking for reliable service, there were no visual cues to inspire confidence.

Through early conversations, a picture emerged. This wasn't about being the cheapest or the flashiest. It was about being the professional choice—someone homeowners could trust to show up, do quality work, and communicate clearly.

I analyzed the competition and identified the gap: modern professionalism without corporate coldness. Approachable without looking amateur.

[Three different logo/visual direction sketches or concepts side by side]

I explored three visual directions—from traditional craftsman aesthetics to sleek minimalism. But only one struck the right balance: confident and clean, with just enough warmth to feel human.

[Close-up of final logo mark]

The mark: simple, memorable, rooted in nature. A leaf that suggests growth and care without resorting to cliché.

[Color palette swatches with physical materials—paint chips, fabric samples]

Earthy green meets warm neutrals. Colors that feel grounded and reliable, not trendy or aggressive.

[Typography specimen showing primary and secondary fonts in use]

Typography that works on a business card and the side of a truck. Clear, sturdy, no-nonsense.

[Business cards laying on wood surface or held in hand at a work site]

Every touchpoint reinforces the same message: this is a business you can count on.

[Uniform shirt with embroidered logo, laid flat or being worn]

From the first handshake, the brand is doing its job.

[Website mockup on laptop screen]

A website that makes booking easy and showcases the work without unnecessary fuss.

[Instagram grid mockup showing consistent branded content]

Social presence built around seasonal tips and project documentation. Helpful, not salesy.

[Wide shot of the branded truck on location, maybe with equipment]

The result? A landscaping business that looks established from day one—giving a new entrepreneur the credibility to compete with companies that have been around for years.

What started as a request for a logo became a comprehensive identity system. Because in a market where professionalism is rare, looking the part isn't vanity—it's a competitive advantage.
