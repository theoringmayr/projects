Monkeys have escaped the zoo

Be on the lookout. Radioactive monkeys are out on the streets and they're hungry. Hungry for bananas.

Monkeys can't be trusted

Escaped monkeys have caused chaos and fascination throughout history. In 1935, around 175 rhesus macaques broke free from a Long Island menagerie, leading to days of mayhem as they blocked trains, raided fruit stands, and evaded capture, with newspapers nationwide following their antics. More recently, 43 rhesus macaques escaped from a research facility in South Carolina, prompting warnings for residents to secure their homes. The monkeys, described as bold and curious, were eventually recaptured without harm. Such incidents highlight both the intelligence and adaptability of monkeys, as well as the challenges in containing these agile animals.
